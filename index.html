The Trip in Brief
旅のあらまし

Twelve days, four cities,
one unforgettable journey.

From Tokyo's electric pulse to Kyoto's quiet temples and Osaka's street-food haze — this is the shape of the days ahead.

Premier Hotels
東京 · Tokyo

Capital electricity

Shibuya's scramble, Senso-ji's incense and the Skytree's view of a city that never quite stops glowing. Five nights to take it in.

富士山 · Fuji-side

Lakeside & outlets

A day out to Oishi Park on Lake Kawaguchi, with Mt. Fuji watching from the north shore — then Gotemba Premium Outlets for unhurried shopping in the mountain's shadow.

ハリポタ · Harry Potter

Wizards in Toshimaen

A full day inside Warner Bros. Studio Tokyo — the Great Hall, Diagon Alley, Platform 9¾ and a Butterbeer to finish.

USJ · Universal

Super Nintendo World & more

A whole day inside Universal Studios Japan in Osaka — Super Nintendo World, the Wizarding World, Hollywood Dream and the Minions.

新幹線 · Shinkansen

320 km/h between worlds

From Tokyo to Kyoto in under two and a half hours aboard the bullet train — your luggage following separately, so you arrive light.

京都 · Kyoto

A thousand torii

Fushimi Inari's vermillion gates, the golden pavilion of Kinkaku-ji and Arashiyama's whispering bamboo. Old Japan, gently.

奈良 · Nara

Where deer bow back

An afternoon among Japan's oldest capital — Todai-ji's giant Buddha and a thousand sacred deer who'll politely wait for crackers.

大阪 · Osaka

The nation's kitchen

Four nights of takoyaki on Dotonbori, lights on the Glico bridge, and Osaka Castle rising above its moat.

食 · Cuisine

Eleven Indian dinners

Every evening, a curated Indian restaurant — from Gujarati thalis to Indo-Chinese, with Jain options. Familiar comfort at the end of busy days.

Day Seven
07
Sunday · 31 May 2026
Kyoto · Temples
京都の社寺

A thousand vermillion gates.

The classical Kyoto loop — Fushimi Inari's torii tunnels, Kinkaku-ji's gold-leaf pavilion and Arashiyama's whispering bamboo. A long day, but the one you'll remember.

Day 7 · Kyoto temples
09:15meet

Meet your guide

English-speaking guide meets at the hotel lobby. Coach departs at 09:30.

10:00Fushimi

Fushimi Inari Taisha

The famed Senbon Torii — ten thousand vermillion gates climbing the sacred mountain of the rice god. Walk as far up as the family enjoys.

12:30Arashiyama

Arashiyama Bamboo Grove

Fifteen minutes of green-cathedral hush, light filtering through stalks twenty metres tall. The most photographed grove in Japan.

13:00lunch · on own

Lunch in Arashiyama

Yudofu (tofu hot-pot), tempura over rice, or a riverside obanzai set — Kyoto's traditional home-style cuisine.

15:30Kinkaku-ji

Kinkaku-ji · The Golden Pavilion

A 14th-century Zen temple covered in gold leaf, mirrored in its mirror pond. Perhaps the most photographed building in Japan, and rightly so.

17:30Ninja

Ninja Samurai Museum & Basic Experience

A hands-on hour with the secret arts of the shinobi — try on a samurai's armour, throw a shuriken, learn a few stealth steps. The children will be delighted.

19:00dinner

Dinner at Indian restaurant · Shorai Kitchen

Papad, cheese-corn ball, tomato soup, malai kofta, bhindi masala, dal tadka, roti, naan and rice.

20:00return

Return to hotel

Back at the DoubleTree. Pack tonight — we head south to Nara and Osaka in the morning.

Day Eight
08
Monday · 1 June 2026
Kyoto → Nara → Osaka
奈良経由

Where the deer bow back.

Check out of Kyoto and head south to Nara — Japan's first permanent capital, where 1,200 sacred deer wander freely. Onward to Osaka in the evening.

Day 8 · Nara, on to Osaka
09:00meet

Meet your guide · check out

English-speaking guide meets at the hotel lobby. Coach departs at 09:15 for the 60-minute drive south to Nara.

10:30Kimono

Dress up in kimono

Slip into traditional kimono at a Nara dressing studio — a beautiful, photographable way to wander Japan's old capital.

11:00Todai-ji

Todai-ji Temple & Great Buddha

One of the largest wooden buildings in the world, housing a 15-metre bronze Buddha cast in 752 AD. The squeeze-through-the-pillar tradition is said to grant enlightenment.

12:00park

Nara Park & the sacred deer

Buy a packet of shika-senbei (deer crackers) — the deer have learned to bow for them. They will follow you. The children will scream with joy.

13:30lunch · on own

Return kimono · lunch in Nara

Hand back the kimono, then try kakinoha-zushi (sushi wrapped in persimmon leaf) — a Nara specialty.

16:00Osaka

Umeda Sky Building · Floating Garden

Osaka's signature observatory — two towers joined at the top with a glass deck, 173 metres up. The escalator ride between the towers is a thrill in itself.

19:00dinner

Dinner at Indian restaurant · ASHOKA

Papad, samosa, dam aloo, cabbage-capsicum sabzi, dal tadka, jeera rice, roti, naan and kheer. Jain options available.

20:00check-in

Transfer to The Westin Osaka · check in

Heavenly beds and a long, hot shower await. Four nights in Osaka begin here.

Meals

Breakfast · Dinner at Indian restaurant

Deer tip

Bow before offering crackers; they bow back. Keep crackers hidden until ready to give.

Good to Know
大切なご案内

Important travel notes.

A handful of things to know before you go — and during the days you are here.

Bus lawDriver hours regulation

By Japan's Ministry of Land, Infrastructure, Transport & Tourism, the bus may operate a maximum of 10 hours per day, between 09:00 and 20:00 only. The driver must receive a minimum 13-hour rest between duties. This regulation is for passenger safety and cannot be altered. Plan late evenings via taxi or train.

Hotel taxPay at check-in

Japan's city / hotel tax is paid directly at the hotel at check-in. Approximate per-person, per-night:
· Tokyo · ¥100 – 200
· Kyoto · ¥200 – 500
· Osaka · ¥100 – 300
Subject to local government and hotel policies.

TicketsShipping & handling

If any ticket arrangements are included or added later, an additional JPY 5,000 will be charged for shipping and handling.

WeatherLate May / early June

Pleasant — 18 – 26°C, lengthening days. Late May edges into rainy season (tsuyu) in Kyoto and Osaka — a compact umbrella is wise. Tokyo stays drier and milder.

TippingNot customary

Tipping is unusual in Japan and may even cause confusion. A small gratuity for your guide and driver at the end of the tour is appreciated but at your discretion.

HealthWalking & pace

This tour involves significant walking (often 8 – 12 km per day) and many steps at temples and shrines. Comfortable shoes are essential. Let your guide know of any mobility needs.

Before You Go
日本の基本

Japan essentials.

The little things — currency, connectivity, culture — that make the trip feel effortless.

¥

Currency

Japan runs on cash more than you'd expect. Carry yen — ATMs at 7-Eleven and Japan Post accept international cards.

1 INR ≈ ¥1.85 (approx, May 2026) · Cards accepted at most hotels, larger shops & restaurants
📶

SIM & Wi-Fi

Buy a Japan-only travel SIM or eSIM at the airport — Sakura Mobile, Mobal or IIJmio are reliable. Pocket Wi-Fi for the group is another option.

Tip · Hotels offer free Wi-Fi; for buses and trains, a SIM is invaluable
⌚

Time & power

Japan Standard Time is 3h 30m ahead of India. Power sockets are Type A (two flat pins, like the US), 100V — carry a universal adapter.

JST · UTC+9 · No daylight saving
語

Language

English signage is reliable in major stations and tourist sites. Outside that, Google Translate's camera mode is your friend. Learn three words:

Sumimasen · Excuse me / sorry · Arigatou · Thank you · Onegaishimasu · Please
礼

Etiquette

Shoes off indoors at temples, ryokan and many restaurants. Don't eat while walking. Don't tip. Bow when in doubt — a small nod is enough.

Chopsticks · Never stick them upright in rice; never pass food chopstick-to-chopstick
食

Food for vegetarians

Vegetarian options exist but require care — dashi (fish stock) is in many dishes. Your guide will help. Pure-veg restaurants are noted on the route.

"Niku nashi de onegai shimasu" · Without meat, please
📷

Photography

Mostly welcome. Avoid taking photographs of geisha in Gion without consent. Some temple interiors prohibit photos — look for signs.

Drones · Banned in most urban areas — leave them at home
🚇

Trains

Tap-in / tap-out Suica or Pasmo IC cards make trains effortless. Your guide can help acquire these on Day 1 if you'd like.

Etiquette · No phone calls on trains · stand left on escalators (right in Osaka)
⛩

Shrines & temples

Shinto shrines have torii gates; Buddhist temples have bells. Wash hands at the chōzuya, bow at the threshold, drop a coin and clap twice at the offering box.

Quiet voices are appreciated everywhere — especially in gardens and shrines
